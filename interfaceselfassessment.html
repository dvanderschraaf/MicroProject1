Interface System — Self-Assessment — Week 4 - 04/02/26

My Figma Page

I think that my interface system deserves a B because of the structure and the clear navigation. There are some minor gaps in feedback and system legibility though.

I think that one of the strongest aspects in my system is the hierarchy and the page structure. Across both mobile and desktop. On the home pages, I organized the content by category. I have the main micro project with the p5 sketches and readings underneath. In the mobile layout, the specific week can be chosen on a drop down menu when pressing the "sketches" button so that the space isnt overwhelming, along with the readings. There is a H1-H4 hierarchy where the page titles(h1), subtitles (h2), buttons (h3) and body text (h4). This supports Nielsons heuristic of recognition rather than recall because the user can visually scan and locate what they need without needing to remember where things are.

My navigation model is also very consistent across all the pages. The top navigation bar appears on both the desktop and mobile versions, practically the same, just layed out a little differently. This creates a predictable system. This aligns with Nielsons consistency and standards heurestic and Schneidermans rule of striving for consistency. I also used the back button on mobile and persistent navigation options on subpages to reduce confusion and support reversible actions.

For affordances and signifiers, ive made interaction areas visually distinct through boxed sections and lebeled sub sections liek "instructions" and "descriptions". This reflects Normans principles, specifically the idea that users should be able to perceive what actions are possible. For example, on the Micro Project page, the sketch canvas is clearly the dominant interactive element, while the instructions and system statues is placed underneath signaling how the system should be used.

One weakness that my system has is the feedback and visibility of system status. Specifically in how dynamic behavior is communicated. While I do include the labeled sections, these are structurally seperated frim the actual sketch itself. According to Nielsons heuristic of visibilty of system status, feedback should be immmidiate and closely tued to the users interactions. In my current layout, users may rely too much on the sketch visuals instead of the laveled data. This craetes a slight disconnect between system behavior and the interface communication.

From a contraints perspective, my interface mostly uses soft contraints rather than explicit ones. For example, navigation is limited to visible links, and interaction is guided through instrunctions rather than inforced instructions. While this does support exploration, it can also introduce uncertainty. Nielsons error prevention heuristic suggests that stronger constraints or clearer guidance could reduce the confusion, especially for begginers encountering the sketches or the site as a whole.

Looking at interation, there is clear evidence of structural development from earlier versions. Initially, content and interaction wwere combined into a single page. In the updated pages, I seperated the sustem into diffeerent pages(home, mircro project hub, the different weeks/sketches, and the different readings). This seperation improves clarity and aligns with Schneidermans emphasis on organizing interfaces into meaningful, predictable structures.

Overall the system demonstrates a strong structure, hierarchym and strong navigation. There is always room for improvement in the feedback clarity and deeper system legibility, though, if I were to refine this further, I would focus on integrating system statues more directly into the sketches themselves. I would also play with scrolling instead of static pages with multiple pages.

This is why I think I deserve a B.
