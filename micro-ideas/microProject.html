Micro-Project — Progress #1

Concept Summary

This interaction models exhaustion through movement. The user controls a rectangle by moving the mouse, and movement drains an energy variable over time. As energy decreases, the rectangle becomes smaller and slower to respond. If the user stops moving, the energy slowly regenerates.

Primary Variable

let energy = 100;

Energy changes based on mouse movement. It controls the size and responsiveness of the rectangle.

Base Build

The first version includes two states: Active and Exhausted. When energy is above a threshold, the rectangle follows the mouse normally. When energy drops below the threshold, movement becomes slow and restricted.

Structural Growth

This system could grow by adding time-based decay, recovery limits, escalation after repeated exhaustion, or temporary lock states. Over time, the imbalance between depletion and recovery could become more visible and structural.

New Changes (02/28/26):
